Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While climate change is a natural phenomenon, human activities have been the main driver since the 1800s, primarily due to the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas. The burning of fossil fuels generates greenhouse gas emissions that act like a blanket wrapped around the Earth, trapping the sun's heat and raising temperatures. The main greenhouse gases include carbon dioxide and methane. The consequences of climate change include intense droughts, water scarcity, severe fires, rising sea levels, flooding, melting polar ice, catastrophic storms, and declining biodiversity.
