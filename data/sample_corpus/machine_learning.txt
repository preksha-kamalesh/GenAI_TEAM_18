Machine learning is a subset of artificial intelligence that focuses on building systems that learn from data. Instead of being explicitly programmed, these systems improve their performance on tasks through experience. Common types include supervised learning, where models learn from labeled data, unsupervised learning, where models find patterns in unlabeled data, and reinforcement learning, where agents learn by interacting with an environment. Popular algorithms include linear regression, decision trees, random forests, support vector machines, and neural networks. Deep learning, a subset of machine learning, uses artificial neural networks with multiple layers to model complex patterns in large datasets.
